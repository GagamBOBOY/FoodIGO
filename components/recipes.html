Chicken Adobo

A savory, slightly tangy chicken dish braised in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and bay leaves.

Click to view

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken thighs
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup cane vinegar
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp oil

Steps

  1. Marinate chicken with soy sauce, garlic, and peppercorns for 30 minutes.
  2. Sear chicken in oil until lightly browned.
  3. Add vinegar, bay leaves, sugar, and enough water to cover. Simmer 30 minutes.
  4. Reduce sauce until slightly thickened. Serve with steamed rice.

Pork Sinigang

A comforting sour soup made with pork, tamarind broth, and fresh vegetables.

Click to view

Ingredients

  • 500 g pork belly, cubed
  • 1 packet tamarind soup mix
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 150 g kangkong or spinach
  • 2 long green chilies

Steps

  1. Boil pork with onion and tomato until tender, skimming foam.
  2. Add tamarind mix and simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Add radish and chilies, cook until soft.
  4. Stir in kangkong or spinach, then remove from heat immediately.

Pork Sisig

A sizzling, tangy dish made with chopped pork, onion, chili, and calamansi.

Click to view

Ingredients

  • 400 g pork belly or liempo
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise (optional)
  • 1-2 bird's eye chilies, sliced
  • 2 calamansi or lemon wedges

Steps

  1. Boil pork until tender, then grill or fry until lightly crisp. Chop into small pieces.
  2. Sauté garlic and onion until soft, then add pork and toss in soy sauce and vinegar.
  3. Add chilies, mayonnaise if using, and cook until coated.
  4. Serve on a hot plate with calamansi squeezed over the top.
